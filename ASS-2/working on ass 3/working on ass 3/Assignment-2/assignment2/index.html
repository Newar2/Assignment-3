Types of Trout Fish

Trout come in several species (and subspecies), each of which differs slightly from the others in appearance. But all of the different species have elongated bodies that are frequently covered with tiny dots. They can be anywhere from dark brown to silver in colour, and many of them also have pink, red, or yellow undertones. Furthermore, size varies greatly between species. Some reach lengths of four feet or more, although the majority are only a foot or two long

Lake Trout

Lake trout are the only major native sport fish adapted to the deep cold water of oligotrophic(low-nutrient) lakes. The lifespan of this type is 20-25 years. The main diet for this fish are crustaceans and insects.They are 61-91 cm in length.This species is frequently caught, especially when fly fishing. In fact, because people like to fish for this species so much, they have introduced it outside of its native habitat in Europe. This fish is currently available in places like New Zealand, Patagonia, and The United States

Rainbow Trout

This fish, another species that is well-liked by fisherman, gets its name from the iridescent tones on its scales. Rainbow trout is a species of salmoid fish generally blue-green or olive green with heavy black spotting over the length of the body.It is sometimes used as an indicator for water quality in water purification facilities. The lifespan of this type is 4-6 years.They are carnivore and their main diet are aquatic insects and terrestrial insects. They are 51-76 cm in length.15 distinct subspecies are now recognised by researchers. Both look and behaviour are different in these subspecies. Others just return to freshwater to reproduce while some live their entire life there.

Brook Trout

The brook trout (Salvelinus fontinalis) is a species of freshwater fish in the char genus Salvelinus of the salmon family Salmonidae. The lifespan of this type is 2-3 years.They are 23-35 cm in length. Adult brook trout have been known to eat insects, worms, crustaceans, leeches, amphibians, small fish, small mammals, and even plants.This species is bred by people using lake trout. Their hybrid progeny, referred to as "splake" or "slake," have distinctive scale patterns and develop quite swiftly. Splake can successfully reproduce, unlike many hybrids, but it doesn't happen often.

Cutthroot Trout

The cutthroat trout (Oncorhynchus clarkii) is a fish species of the family Salmonidae native to cold-water tributaries of the Pacific Ocean, Rocky Mountains, and Great Basin in North America.Its lifespan is 6-8 years.It is 15 -102 cm long.Their favorite meals include insects, fish eggs, snails, salmon fry, herring, shrimp, krill, and squid.The reddish colour of the scales under its chin makes it easy to identify this species, hence the name "cutthroat." This species, which is the state fish of Wyoming, inhabits freshwater habitats in the Pacific Northwest. 15 distinct subspecies are recognised by researchers.
